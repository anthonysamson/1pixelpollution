Hope for the Future

While global CO₂ emissions are high, renewable energy and personal actions are making a difference.

In 2023, Renewables generated ~33% of global electricity.
 Based on the estimated jump to ~46% in 2030⁸, that means we should see ~13% decrease in non-renewables usage.
Lets apply this to Boone North Carolina's CO₂ contribution.




The dark blue section represents the amount of CO₂ reduced by the additional usage of renewables.
If all 20,000 people in Boone either walked, biked, used public transit, or switched to EVs
we could save ~43,680 tonnes of CO₂ per year.⁹




The light blue section represents the amount of CO₂ reduced by better transportation usage.
If all 20,000 people in Boone switched to reusable cups and containers we could save ~20,000 tonnes of CO₂ per year.¹⁰




The light purple section represents the amount of CO₂ reduced by better transportation usage.
By eating more plant-based meals (3 meat-free days/week), carbon emissions can be reduced even further
by around 11,400 tonnes of CO₂ a year.¹¹




The pink section represents the amount of CO₂ reduced by better eating habits.
By implementing more recycling and composting we can save an additional ~6,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually.¹²




The tan section represents the amount of CO₂ reduced by better recycling and reduction of single-use plastics.¹²
Combined CO₂ reductions for Boone from renewables, transportation, reusable cups, plant-based meals, and recycling.
All together, this accounts for a 31% total decrease in CO₂ emissions.

The Power of Action

From a single person emitting 5 tonnes of CO₂ per year, to major corporations like Shell releasing billions of tonnes, the scale of global emissions can feel overwhelming.

However, the example from here in Boone, North Carolina, shows that both renewable energy and everyday actions can make a measurable difference:

  • Transitioning to renewable electricity in just one town can reduce tens of thousands of tonnes of CO₂ annually.
  • Individual choices such as walking, biking, using public transit, switching to EVs, eating more plant-based meals,
    using reusable containers, and recycling, collectively reduce thousands of tonnes of CO₂ each year.

These small but consistent actions, multiplied across communities and countries, add up to significant reductions.
The visualizations in this site illustrate that every pixel of effort counts.

Ultimately, while historical emissions are vast, the future is not fixed. With widespread adoption of renewable energy, sustainable lifestyles, and mindful corporate policies,
we have the power to flatten emissions curves and move toward a more sustainable world.

Every action matters, and together, we can make a visible impact.
